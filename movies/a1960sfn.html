Star Wars (original trilogy) (1977-1983)

Star Wars is an epic space opera franchise created by George Lucas. It depicts the adventures of characters "a long time ago in a galaxy far, far away". The franchise began in 1977 with the release of Star Wars: A New Hope. It was followed by the sequels The Empire Strikes Back (1980) and Return of the Jedi (1983); these three films constitute the original Star Wars trilogy. The trilogy stars Mark Hamill, Harrison Ford, Carrie Fisher, Alec Guinness, and James Earl Jones and focuses on the Rebel Alliance, led by Princess Leia (Fisher), and its attempt to battle the Galactic Empire, led by Darth Vader and the Emperor. Central characters include Luke Skywalker, Han Solo, Chewbacca, Yoda, R2-D2 and C3-P0.
