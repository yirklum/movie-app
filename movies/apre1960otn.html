Wings (1927)

Wings is a silent film set during the First World War starring Clara Bow, Charles "Buddy" Rogers, and Richard Arlen. In the film two young men, one rich, one middle class, who are in love with the same woman, become fighter pilots in World War I. Acclaimed for its technical prowess and realism upon release, the film became the yardstick against which future aviation films were measured, mainly because of its realistic air-combat sequences. It went on to win the first Academy Award for Best Picture.
