The Birdcage (La Cage Aux Folles) (1978)

The Birdcage (La Cage aux Folles) is a Franco-Italian comedy film and the first film adaptation of Jean Poiret's 1973 play. The film tells the story of a gay couple – Renato Baldi (Ugo Tognazzi), the manager of a Saint-Tropez nightclub featuring drag entertainment, and Albin Mougeotte (Michel Serrault), his star attraction – and the madness that ensues when Renato's son, Laurent (Rémi Laurent), brings home his fiancée, Andrea (Luisa Maneri), and her ultra-conservative parents (Carmen Scarpitta and Michel Galabru) to meet them.
