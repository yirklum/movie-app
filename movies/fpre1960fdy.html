The Bicycle Thief (1948)

The Bicycle Thief (Ladri di biciclette) is an film directed by Vittorio De Sica. The film follows the story of a poor father searching post-World War II Rome for his stolen bicycle, without which he will lose the job which was to be the salvation of his young family. It stars Lamberto Maggiorani as the desperate father and Enzo Staiola as his plucky young son. The Bicycle Thief is widely regarded as a masterpiece of Italian neorealism.
